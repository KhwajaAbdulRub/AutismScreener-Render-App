Know More About Autism

What is Autism?

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a developmental condition that affects how a person communicates, interacts socially, and behaves. It’s called a "spectrum" because the severity and combination of symptoms can vary widely from one individual to another.

Autism typically appears in early childhood, often before age 3, and can impact a child’s ability to engage with others, express themselves, or adapt to changes. While there’s no cure, early intervention can significantly improve outcomes.

Possible Causes and Risk Factors

Genetic Factors

  • Family history of autism
  • Genetic mutations or variations
  • Associated with conditions like Fragile X syndrome

Environmental Factors

  • Prenatal exposure to certain toxins
  • Advanced parental age
  • Complications during pregnancy or birth

Diagnosis Process

Diagnosing autism involves a multi-step process, typically starting with developmental screening during well-child visits. Pediatricians look for delays in milestones like speech or social engagement.

Screening Tools

  • M-CHAT (Modified Checklist for Autism in Toddlers)
  • ADOS (Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule)
  • Parent and caregiver interviews

Professional Evaluation

  • Child psychologists or neurologists
  • Speech and language assessments
  • Behavioral observations

Common Myths About Autism

Debunking Misconceptions
  • Myth: Vaccines cause autism (False - Extensive research disproves this)
  • Myth: Autism only affects children (False - It’s a lifelong condition)
  • Myth: All autistic individuals have the same symptoms (False - It’s a spectrum)
