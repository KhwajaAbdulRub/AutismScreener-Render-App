Understanding Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent challenges in social communication and interaction, as well as restricted and repetitive behaviors. The term "spectrum" reflects the wide variation in challenges and strengths found among individuals with ASD.

Key Symptoms and Signs

Social Communication and Interaction

  • Difficulty in social-emotional reciprocity
  • Abnormalities in eye contact and body language
  • Challenges in developing relationships

Restricted and Repetitive Behaviors

  • Repetitive movements or speech
  • Insistence on sameness
  • Highly fixated interests

Treatment and Management

Behavioral Interventions

  • Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA)
  • Speech Therapy
  • Occupational Therapy
  • Social Skills Training

Support Services

  • Parent training programs
  • Educational support
  • Vocational training
  • Community resources

Early Intervention

Why Early Detection Matters
  • Better developmental outcomes
  • Earlier access to support services
  • Improved social and communication skills
  • Enhanced quality of life
